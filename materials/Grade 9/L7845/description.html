Title: Trig degrees: cosine to 360 
Description: Use a unit circle tool to explore cosine values in different quadrants. Determine the cos Ø values for angles between 0° and 360°. Observe the symmetry and patterns of the functions to predict the behaviour of cos Ø in different quadrants. Observe the graph animate and visually connect it with the unit circle tool and table values. Extend cos Ø for angles beyond 360°. Find out how trigonometry relates to the real world. This learning object is one in a series of nine objects. Three objects in the series are also packaged as a combined learning object. 
Topics: Cosine; Function graphs; Patterns; Right angled triangles; Sine; Symmetry; Trigonometry 
Keywords: Cosine, Cosine function, Function graphs, Patterns, Periodic phenomena, Quadrants, Right angled triangles, Right-angled triangles, Sine, Symmetry, Trigonometry, Unit circle 
Learning area: Mathematics

Key learning objectives:
  • Students apply the unit circle definitions of the cosine function.
  • Students interpret a graph of cos Ø, from 0° to 360°.
  • Students identify patterns and symmetry of the cosine function.
Educational value:
  • Enables students to compare and understand the key features of the cosine function (from 0° to 360°).
  • Provides an interactive unit circle tool to ensure students work with general definitions of the cosine function, connected to the right-angled triangle definition.
  • Includes challenges that highlight the symmetry and patterns of the cosine function.
  • Contains associations with real-world periodic phenomena that are modelled with trigonometric functions.
Student activity: Analysis; Data manipulation and interpretation; Experiment; Interactives; Modelling; Multiple choice questions
